God of War (2018)

Action-adventure Mythic narrative Single-player

Lore

Wikipedia states that this entry shifts the series from Greek mythology to Norse mythology, mainly in Midgard. After the death of Faye, Kratos and his son Atreus begin a journey to spread her ashes at the highest peak of the nine realms.

The story explores father-son tension, hidden divine identity, and conflict with Norse gods and creatures. The narrative is directly tied to the game's world progression across multiple realms.

Gameplay

  • Third-person action-adventure with an over-the-shoulder camera and "one-shot" presentation style.
  • Kratos uses the Leviathan Axe and can combine attacks with Atreus support abilities.
  • Includes RPG-style progression, gear upgrades, optional challenges, and secret bosses like Valkyries.

World and Setting Details

Wikipedia notes that the game explores multiple realms of Norse mythology rather than being locked to one location. Midgard is the central hub, while major story travel includes realms like Alfheim, Helheim, and Jotunheim.

This realm-hopping structure supports both narrative pacing and gameplay variety, especially with optional challenge spaces.

Combat Identity

  • Leviathan Axe combat focuses on weighty hits, recall timing, and deliberate positioning.
  • Atreus functions as tactical support, helping with crowd control and combo extension.
  • RPG elements encourage different builds through armor, upgrades, and runic ability choices.
